The Information Gap Is Just Time Travel for Rich Countries

🆕 NEW PREMISE 💡 Thesis

From Jesse's 4/5 diary: his American friend was doing multi-agent orchestration and context engineering last October. Jesse just heard about it. Same industry, same year, six-month lag. Concept collision: information gap = living in the same year but different timelines. China tech workers are playing the American tech meta from two patches ago.

  1. My friend in the US was doing multi-agent orchestration in October. I discovered it in April. We work in the same industry. I'm not behind — I'm in a different season of the same show.🔥
  2. The information gap between China and the US isn't a wall. It's a spoiler delay. Americans are on Episode 10, I'm on Episode 4, and LinkedIn keeps showing me the ending.🔥
  3. She made principal in four years. I've been a designer for five and my biggest promotion was getting added to a Slack channel.🔥
  4. "Context engineering" — that's a real job title now. In China we just call that "reading the room." Americans will name anything and get promoted for it.🔥
  5. The tech industry runs on information asymmetry. Whoever learns the new thing first wins. It's like a gold rush except the gold is a GitHub repo and the rush is anxiety.

Charisma Is a Cheat Code I Never Unlocked

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🎯 Punch

From Jesse's 4/5 diary: "优秀的人都有一种能量" — great people have this energy. His friend's facial expressions alone give emotional value. She says normal things and they sound exciting. Jesse wonders: what's different? Concept collision: charisma = emotional auto-tune. Some people's default frequency is inspiring. Yours is buffering.

  1. Some people walk into a room and the energy shifts. I walk into a room and the energy stays exactly the same. I'm a human screensaver.🔥
  2. My friend can say "I had lunch" and it sounds like a TED talk. I can present a quarterly strategy and it sounds like I'm reading a receipt.🔥
  3. Charisma is just auto-tune for your personality. Some people are born with it. The rest of us are doing standup trying to install it manually.🔥
  4. She said "don't get satisfaction from proving others wrong." That's easy to say when you're a principal at 28. That's like a billionaire saying money doesn't matter.🔥
  5. Every time I talk to someone successful I feel inspired for exactly 45 minutes. Then my brain recalculates the gap and I feel worse than before. Inspiration is an emotional payday loan.🔥

My Dad Told Me to Get a Foreign Passport Like It's a Costco Card

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🧱 Build

From Jesse's 4/5 diary: dad and uncle casually said "搞一个国外的护照" — get a foreign passport. Australia or New Zealand. Like they were recommending a restaurant. Jesse was shocked because he already wanted to go abroad but never told them. Concept collision: Chinese parents suggesting emigration = the ultimate risk assessment. When your parents tell you to leave, the country isn't the one with the problem — your career is.

  1. My dad said "get a foreign passport" the same way he'd say "get more vegetables." Just casual life advice. Emigrate. Eat greens. Same energy.🔥
  2. Chinese parents don't say "I love you." They say "have you considered New Zealand." Same thing.🔥
  3. When your Chinese parents tell you to emigrate, that's not advice — that's a national risk assessment from the most conservative investors on earth.🔥
  4. My uncle recommended Australia like he was reviewing it on Yelp. "Good weather, good schools, easy visa." Four stars. Would emigrate again.🔥
  5. I've been secretly wanting to move abroad for years. My dad brought it up first. Turns out my exit strategy had a exit strategy.🔥

💡 Concept Seeds (for Jesse to grow)

  • Information Gap × Spoiler Culture: Living in China tech is watching a show on delay. You already know the ending from Twitter. The suspense isn't "what happens" — it's "when does it happen to me." Your career is a movie you've already been spoiled on.
  • Charisma × Compression Algorithm: Charismatic people compress complex ideas into emotional bandwidth. They don't say more — they transmit more per word. You have the same data but your codec is unoptimized. Standup is learning to compress.
  • Passport × Insurance Policy: A second passport is life insurance. You're not betting against your country — you're hedging. Chinese parents invented diversification before Wall Street. They just call it "backup plan" instead of "portfolio strategy."
  • Inspiration × Sugar Rush: Talking to successful people = eating candy. Instant energy, zero nutrition, crash in an hour. The real work happens after the crash, when you're alone with your actual life and no one else's highlight reel.
  • Principal at 28 × Speedrun: Some people are speedrunning careers. You're on a casual playthrough. Different games. Except LinkedIn puts everyone's score on the same leaderboard and you can't turn off notifications.
