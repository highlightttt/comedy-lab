Premise 01 — Gym Fixed Nothing, Added Problems

🆕 New Premise

You work out for a year to fix your neck. Result: now your neck AND your knees hurt. Exercise is a subscription service where the bugs are features.

  1. I exercised for a year to fix my neck. The X-ray came back the same. My body treated the whole year like a free trial it didn't convert on.🔥
  2. My doctor said "keep exercising, it helps long-term." That's what my manager says about unpaid overtime.
  3. I went to the gym to fix one problem. Now I have three. That's not fitness — that's a Jira board for my skeleton.🔥
  4. My neck is the same. My knees are worse. My wallet is lighter. But hey — my biceps look great in the X-ray.
  5. Exercise is a pyramid scheme. You put in pain now for "future gains." But the only thing gaining is the number of joints that crack when I stand up.

Premise 02 — After Chest Day, I Understand Women

💡 Expanded from 4/8

Heavy bench press gave me empathy I never asked for. Post chest day, every bump hurts. I finally understand bras — not the clasp, the necessity. The gym is the only gender studies class men voluntarily attend.

  1. After chest day, I bumped into a door frame and almost cried. That's not pain — that's a women's studies elective I didn't sign up for.🔥
  2. I used to think bras were decorative. After heavy bench, I understand — they're structural engineering. Load-bearing architecture.
  3. Ladies, if your boyfriend doesn't understand what you go through — make him do chest day. If he still doesn't get it, make him do chest day in a sports bra.
  4. The gym is the only place where a man can develop empathy through physical trauma. Everywhere else, we just ignore it.
  5. I told my girlfriend "I finally understand what you deal with." She said "PMS?" I said "No. Stairs after chest day."🔄 callback

Premise 03 — Chinese Gym vs American Gym

🆕 New Premise — Blue Ocean

Chinese gym culture: grandpas doing pull-ups in the park at 6am with no shirt on. American gym culture: 25-year-olds paying $200/month to do the same thing but with Bluetooth. The equipment is different, the shirtlessness is the same.

  1. In China, the fittest people are 70-year-old men doing pull-ups in the park for free. In America, the fittest people are 25-year-olds paying $200/month to watch themselves do it in a mirror.🔥
  2. Chinese parks have more gym equipment than American gyms. The difference? No WiFi, no protein shake bar, no one filming a reel. Just results.
  3. My grandpa's workout: carry water up a hill. My workout: carry guilt to the gym and back. He's 80 and fine. I'm 32 and my neck doesn't work.🔄 callback P1
  4. American gyms play motivational music. Chinese park gyms play 广场舞 music. One makes you lift heavier. The other makes you dance whether you want to or not.
  5. In China, going shirtless at the gym is just Tuesday. In America, going shirtless at the gym is a thirst trap with a caption about "the grind."🆕
