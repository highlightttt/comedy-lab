Cultural Appropriation × Work Culture

🆕 NEW PREMISE 💡 Thesis

Jesse's original seed: China has 996 but no word for "work-life balance." America has "work-life balance" but nobody does it. Both imported the other's worst idea and called it progress. Concept collision: work-culture exchange as the trade war nobody talks about.

  1. China exported 996 to Silicon Valley startups. America exported "work-life balance" to Chinese LinkedIn. Neither side read the instructions.🔥
  2. "Work-life balance" in Chinese offices means you balance your laptop on your lap while living on a cot in the break room.
  3. America invented "hustle culture." China invented 996. Same disease, different branding. One has a podcast, the other has a government investigation.🔥
  4. The real tariff war isn't steel or soybeans. It's toxic work concepts. America tariffed our products, we tariffed their weekends.🔥
  5. Chinese companies now say "we value work-life balance." American startups now say "we're a family." Both sentences mean "you're working Saturday."🔥
  6. 996 is honest. 9am to 9pm, 6 days. You know the deal. "Unlimited PTO" is the American version — sounds generous until you realize taking it gets you fired. At least 996 doesn't gaslight you.🔥

Your Manager Is Your Long-Distance Partner

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🎯 Punch

WFH turned every manager-report relationship into a long-distance relationship. Daily standups = good morning texts. Camera on = "let me see your face." RTO = "I need you to move closer." Concept collision: corporate hierarchy as romantic dependency.

  1. WFH turned my manager into my long-distance girlfriend. Daily standups are "good morning" texts. "Camera on, please" is "let me see your face, I don't trust you."🔥
  2. RTO is just your company saying "I think we should close the distance." Same guilt trip. Same ultimatum. Same tears.🔥
  3. "Are you still there?" — something said by both Zoom and a neglected partner.
  4. Slack status "Away" hits different when your manager has trust issues. That's not a status — that's evidence.🔥
  5. "Let's have a quick sync" is corporate for "we need to talk." Both ruin your afternoon.🔥
  6. My manager sends me a "?" when I don't reply in 10 minutes. That's not management. That's the energy of someone checking your location.

Permission Structure: Teaching Audiences to Laugh

🆕 STAGE CRAFT 🧱 Build

From Jesse's stage note: the homeless bit made people "不敢笑." The joke worked, but the audience needed permission. This is a meta-comedy premise AND a practical tool. Concept collision: laughter as a crime that needs a permit.

  1. The best dark jokes aren't the ones that make you laugh. They're the ones that make you look around first to see if it's okay to laugh. Laughter needs a hall pass.🔥
  2. When the audience goes quiet after a dark joke, that's not failure. That's them laughing internally and waiting for a lawyer to approve the release.🔥
  3. In China, you laugh at the government joke AFTER you check who's sitting next to you. In America, you laugh at the race joke AFTER you check who's sitting next to you. Same muscle, different stakes.🔥
  4. A comedian's real job isn't writing jokes. It's being the first person to say the terrible thing out loud so everyone else can finally laugh about it. We're emotional bomb disposal.🔥
  5. "You can't say that!" is the highest compliment in comedy. It means you said what everyone was thinking and they're mad you got there first.
  6. The trick to the homeless joke: laugh at it yourself first. You're not giving them a punchline — you're giving them a permission slip. Sign it with your smile.🧱 STAGE TIP

  • Performance Review × Relationship: "We need to discuss your growth this quarter" = "Where is this relationship going?" Annual review = anniversary dinner where one person cries.
  • Unlimited PTO × Chinese New Year: Americans have unlimited PTO and take 5 days. Chinese have 7 days of CNY and work 2 weekends to "pay it back." Both systems designed to make you feel guilty for resting.
  • "Two Offices" Fix: Jesse's "you don't have home, you have 2 offices" — try: "one office has a microwave, the other has a bed. Only difference? One pays rent." Lean into the word "office" until home stops feeling like home.
  • Permission × Immigration: Immigrants already live inside a permission structure. Green card = permission to exist. Work permit = permission to earn. Comedy is the only place where I give the permissions now.
  • Trade War × Vocabulary: America exports words: "disruption," "pivot," "synergy." China exports numbers: 996, 007, 715. The real trade deficit is linguistic — we're importing their bullshit at a 3:1 ratio.
