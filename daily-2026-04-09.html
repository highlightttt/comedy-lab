"Smart" Doesn't Describe the Device — It Describes the Entry Requirement

🆕 NEW PREMISE 💡 Thesis

From #comedy 4/8: "Smart" in smart phone means YOU have to be smart enough to use it. Dad got a smart TV and stopped watching — 3 remotes. Smart rice cooker you have to talk to — introvert nightmare. The smarter your house gets, the dumber you feel. Concept collision: "smart home" = your house judging you.

  1. "Smart" in smartphone doesn't describe the phone. It describes the minimum IQ required to operate it.🔥
  2. My dad got a smart TV. He hasn't watched television since. Three remotes. He went from 200 channels to zero.🔥
  3. Now there's a smart rice cooker you have to talk to. I'm an introvert — I didn't sign up for roommates I have to speak to. I was enjoying my alone time. Now my kitchen has social expectations.🔥
  4. A smart home is just a house that's smarter than you. You're the dumbest thing in your own apartment and you paid for the upgrade.🔥
  5. Every "smart" product is a literacy test disguised as a purchase. The fridge has Wi-Fi. I don't know why. But if I can't connect it, I'm the idiot.

ADHD People Are Apex Predators Stuck in Spreadsheets

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🎯 Punch

From #comedy 4/7: ChatGPT says ADHD people are "gifted" — good at high-stimulation tasks with instant feedback and danger. Translation: hunting, killing, and scrolling your phone before sleep. Concept collision: ADHD = a prehistoric operating system running on modern hardware.

  1. ChatGPT told me ADHD people are gifted. We excel at tasks with high stimulation, instant feedback, and danger. So: hunting, killing, and scrolling TikTok at 2 AM.🔥
  2. ADHD is a prehistoric operating system running on a modern laptop. Great at dodging tigers. Terrible at quarterly reports.🔥
  3. I'm ADHD, raised by parents who work in school. Hardworking but useless — like a hamster wheel connected to nothing.🔥
  4. We're gifted. Just not at anything that pays.🔥
  5. ADHD people would've been the village hero 10,000 years ago. Now we're the guy who opened 47 browser tabs and forgot why.

When You Answer Your Dad Like a Zoom Call

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🧱 Build

From #comedy 4/8: Dad asks "why are you using so much toilet paper?" Response: "Dad, that's a great question. Thanks for raising it." Corporate meeting language as a defense mechanism at home. Concept collision: the corporate accent is the new fight-or-flight.

  1. My dad asked why I use so much toilet paper. I said, "Dad, that's a great question. Thanks for raising it." He looked at me like I was possessed.🔥
  2. Corporate speak is the new fight-or-flight. Someone yells at you, your brain doesn't panic — it generates a follow-up email.🔥
  3. "Let's circle back on this" is the adult version of "I don't know, ask your mother."🔥
  4. You know you've worked too long at a corporation when your mom calls and you say "let me put some time on your calendar."🔥
  5. "Per my last text, Mom, I already told you I ate." That's not a son. That's a Slack notification with legs.🔥

💡 Concept Seeds (for Jesse to grow)

  • Smart Device × Age Gap: Every smart product creates a generation filter. Smart TV killed dad's TV habit. Smart lock means grandma can't visit unannounced anymore. Technology isn't advancing — it's gatekeeping. "You must be this tech-literate to enter your own home."
  • ADHD × Modern Jobs: Every job description is the opposite of ADHD strengths. "Detail-oriented, self-motivated, good at sitting still for 8 hours." That's not a job — that's a punishment. We built a civilization optimized for the neurotype that would've died first in the wild.
  • Corporate × Dating: Dating apps are just corporate culture for romance. "Let's schedule a sync" = first date. "We should align on expectations" = the talk. "I'm pursuing other opportunities" = breakup. Ghosting is just "position has been filled internally."
  • Smart Home × Introvert: I bought smart devices to avoid people. Now my house talks more than my coworkers. Alexa won't shut up. The fridge beeps. The doorbell has opinions. I automated my loneliness and got a chatty apartment instead.
