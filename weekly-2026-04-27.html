Premise 01 — Immigration Points = RPG Character Build

💡 Thesis

Australia's skilled visa is just a character creation screen. Age? Losing stats after 33. English? Grind the language dungeon. NAATI CCL? A $800 side quest for +5 points. And you can't even respec.

  1. Australia's 189 visa is an RPG. You min-max your stats, grind side quests, and at the end… you get to live in a country that's trying to kill you with wildlife.🔥
  2. I turn 33 in October. In immigration points, that's "your character starts decaying." I'm not aging — I'm depreciating.🔥
  3. NAATI CCL is a side quest where you pay $800 to prove you speak Chinese. I've been speaking Chinese for 32 years but sure, let me audition for my own language.
  4. My occupation code is 232413 — "Multimedia Designer." I design things at Microsoft. Australia sees that and goes: "Prove it. With paperwork." Immigration is just enterprise software for humans.
  5. New Zealand requires a job offer to immigrate. Australia requires a spreadsheet. I chose the spreadsheet. That's how you know I'm a designer.

Premise 02 — PTE Exam = Tinder for Countries

🎯 Punch

PTE is a dating app where the country swipes on YOU. Score 79+? Match. Score 61? "We'll keep your profile on file." You're not learning English — you're building a résumé for a continent.

  1. I scored 61 on my PTE mock. That's the English equivalent of "he's nice but I'm not feeling it."🔥
  2. I lived in the UK for a year. Worked at Microsoft in English every day. PTE still said 61. Apparently experience doesn't count — only the exam does. It's the SAT of adulthood.
  3. My "Extended Speaking" was 79 but my "Write From Dictation" was garbage. I can give a TED talk but I can't take notes. I'm the guy who inspires you at the conference and forgets his own room number.🔥
  4. They say WFD is "the highest ROI section." Just memorize template sentences. So the path to Australia is… rote memorization. I left China to escape that system and it followed me.
  5. PTE tests you on "repeat sentence." A robot says a sentence, you say it back. That's not an English test — that's how you train a parrot. Australia is accepting parrots now? Makes sense, they have enough of them.

Premise 03 — Portfolio = Existential Particle System

🧱 Build

Jesse's portfolio concept: everything is particles that scatter when you hover and reassemble when you leave. That's not a website — that's a metaphor for his career. Touch it and it falls apart. Wait, and it pretends to be coherent again.

  1. My portfolio is a particle system. Hover over my career and it disintegrates. Leave it alone and it reforms into something that looks intentional. Just like my actual career.🔥
  2. The four design keywords are "Dynamic, Organic, Primitive, Symbiotic." That's not a portfolio — that's a Burning Man camp description.
  3. I'm building a website where text is made of sand. You push it, it scatters. It's interactive art. It's also what happens to my self-esteem during performance reviews.
  4. "Human-AI-viewer symbiosis." I showed my boss. He said "can you make it load faster." Three layers of co-creation collapsed into one layer of disappointment.🔥
  5. I can't decide between 2D Canvas and 3D Spark for my portfolio. That's the designer equivalent of "should I text back or wait three days." Either way I'm overthinking something no one asked for.
