The Hardest Workers Have the Least Holidays

💡 Thesis

New angle on the ethnic makeup / globalization thesis. Not about the office — about the global system. The countries that make everything get the fewest days off. It's not a coincidence. It's a business model. Chinese, Indian, Mexican workers power the world's weekends.

  1. 330 million Americans enjoy holidays because 1.4 billion Chinese work through theirs. That's not globalization — that's outsourced relaxation.🔥💡 Thesis
  2. My team is Chinese, Indian, and Mexican. Three countries famous for hard work and great food. Coincidence? No. You can't cook that good if you have free time — that's desperation seasoning.🔥🎯 Punch
  3. Work-life balance is a spectrum. On one end: Scandinavians, 6-hour days, free healthcare. On the other: my team, replying to Slack at 2 AM like it's a lifestyle choice.🧱 Build
  4. The harder a country works, the better the food. England had an empire and still eats beans on toast. Proof that colonizing doesn't count as effort.🔥🎯 Punch
  5. Americans put tariffs on Chinese goods. But you can't put a tariff on work ethic. That's why you hired us in the first place.🎯 Punch

  • MVP Man × Smart Devices: Men are the only product that gets dumber as it gets more features. Smart TV needs 3 remotes. Smart Man needs 3 apps to remember his girlfriend's birthday.
  • Fridge Archaeology × Career Layers: Your fridge and your resume have the same structure — the deeper you go, the more embarrassing it gets. "2019 me bought quinoa. 2019 me also thought I'd be VP by now."
  • Food Quality × National Output: Countries with the best cuisine have the worst work-life balance. The flavor comes from centuries of "I only have 20 minutes to eat before my next shift."
  • For Men × For Countries: "For Men" removes features and makes it black. "For developing countries" removes labor laws and makes it cheap. Same energy.
  • Cactus Watering Can × Global Economy: You don't build a specialized watering can for a cactus. You also don't build labor protections for workers who'll work anyway. The cactus and the immigrant have the same business model: low maintenance, high output.
