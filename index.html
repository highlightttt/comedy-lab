Original One-Liners
01Americans say no. Chinese say "let me think about it" (they already thought — it's no). Indians say "yes, definitely" (it's also no).
02Three ways to say no in a meeting: American: "No." Chinese: "This is interesting, let's discuss offline." Indian: "Yes, we can do that" — and then nothing happens.

06At Microsoft, "Let's circle back" is American for no. "Let's align offline" is Indian for no. Silence is Chinese for no. Nobody just says no.
07My Chinese colleague said "This proposal is very interesting." I got excited. My American colleague whispered, "That means he hates it."
08Indian head wobble: the original encryption. Only Indians can decrypt it. The rest of us just smile and wait.
09If a Chinese person says "we'll see," cancel the project. If an Indian person says "it's challenging but possible," also cancel the project.
10I pitched an idea. My American boss said no in 2 seconds. My Chinese boss would've let me present for 30 minutes and THEN say no — politely, in an email, two days later.

06Americans: "Nice weather today." Indians: "How's your family? Your parents healthy? Your sister married yet?" Chinese: *opens laptop*

08An Indian colleague I met for 5 minutes knows more about my life than my American friends of 5 years.
09Small talk at Microsoft: Americans talk about sports, Indians talk about visa status, Chinese talk about housing prices. Nobody listens to each other.
10My American colleague said "We should hang out!" three years ago. We have not hung out. My Indian colleague said "Come over Saturday." I went. There were 40 people there. It was a wedding.

10The only day everyone is in the office is the day free food is provided. That's the real universal holiday.
