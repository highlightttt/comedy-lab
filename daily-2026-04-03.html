Learning English = A Relationship That Won't Commit

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🎯 Punch

From Jesse's 3/29 diary: studied in UK, dated a native speaker for 3 years, works at a foreign company — still can't freestyle on stage. Concept collision: English isn't a skill, it's a relationship. Jesse's done everything right and it still won't commit.

  1. I studied in England. I dated an English speaker for three years. I work at an American company. English looked at all that and said "let's just be friends."🔥
  2. I do standup in English. But only the memorized parts. The second someone asks me a question, I become a loading screen with a face.🔥
  3. My English has commitment issues. It shows up for the scripted parts and ghosts me during crowd work.🔥
  4. People say "immerse yourself." I immersed. I studied abroad, dated abroad, worked abroad. English still treats me like a guest.🔥
  5. I blame my memory. I forget every word I learn. My brain is a sieve with a language setting stuck on Mandarin.

My Mood Is a Stock Ticker Controlled by Strangers

🆕 NEW PREMISE 💡 Thesis

From Jesse's 3/29 diary: good set = high for days, bombing = crash for days. Concept collision: comedian's emotional life = day trading. Your portfolio is other people's laughter. You have zero diversification.

  1. When my jokes land, I'm high for a week. When I bomb, I'm depressed for a week. I'm not a comedian — I'm an emotional day trader with no diversification.🔥
  2. Comedy was supposed to be my hobby. Now my self-worth is a stock chart and strangers in a restaurant are the market.🔥
  3. I bombed last week. In a loud restaurant where nobody was listening. That's like losing money on a stock exchange that was closed.🔥
  4. After bombing I don't want to do comedy for weeks. That's not quitting — that's emotional stop-loss kicking in.🔥
  5. Comedy is the only hobby where the fun part makes you suicidal. Nobody goes fishing and comes home like "I'm never casting a line again."🔥

My Therapist Is an AI I Programmed Myself

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🧱 Build

From Jesse's 3/29 diary: after a year of real therapy, he asked his AI assistant to be his quasi-therapist — find patterns, spot irrational beliefs. Concept collision: outsourcing self-awareness to a chatbot. You built a robot to tell you you're crazy.

  1. I did a year of therapy. Then I built an AI and said "you do it." That's not progress — that's automating your breakdown.🔥
  2. My therapist charged $200 an hour. My AI charges per token. I finally disrupted the one industry that was keeping me sane.🔥
  3. I told my AI "find my irrational beliefs." It came back with a list. Number one was "thinking an AI can replace your therapist."🔥
  4. I record voice diaries for my AI to analyze. A robot knows my deepest fears and my ex doesn't even know my birthday. Priorities.🔥
  5. The AI found five behavioral patterns in my diary. My human therapist found two in a year. Either the AI is better or I'm getting worse faster.

💡 Concept Seeds (for Jesse to grow)

  • Language × Dating History: Your English fluency mirrors your relationship pattern — intense effort, long commitment, still ends with "it's not you, it's me." Every language app is basically Tinder for vocabulary.
  • Bombing × Weather Forecast: Comedians should get a weather app for rooms. "Tonight: 80% chance of silence, scattered pity laughs, with a cold front of crossed arms moving in from the back row."
  • AI Therapist × Subscription Model: Therapy used to be a relationship. Now it's a subscription. Next step: AI therapist with a free tier — "You get three insights a month. Upgrade to Premium Trauma for unlimited breakthroughs."
  • Memory × Language: Bad memory in your native language = quirky. Bad memory in your second language = "maybe I'm just stupid." Same brain, different PR.
  • Crowd Work × Job Interview: Crowd work is a job interview where the interviewer is drunk and you applied in a language you barely speak. And the salary is laughter. Which you might not get.
