Coffee Is Liquid Overtime, Tea Is a Liquid Resignation Letter

🆕 NEW PREMISE 💡 Thesis

From #comedy 3/29: In China, drinking tea = enjoying life, coffee = hardworking. Coffee people have careers. Tea people have afternoons. That's why tea is getting less popular — slow living is dying. Concept collision: your beverage is your LinkedIn profile.

  1. Coffee is liquid overtime. Tea is a liquid resignation letter.🔥
  2. In China, if you drink coffee at work, you're hardworking. If you drink tea, people assume you don't have a job — you have a lifestyle.🔥
  3. Tea is losing market share to coffee in China. Slow living isn't dying — it got laid off.🔥
  4. Your drink is your résumé. Espresso? Finance. Matcha latte? Designer. Hot water? You work at a Chinese state-owned enterprise and you'll be there forever.🔥
  5. The office drink hierarchy: coffee means you ship at midnight. Tea means you leave at five. Energy drink means you sleep under the desk. Water means you've given up on all stimulation including hope.

Your Company's Ethnic Makeup Predicts Your Weekend

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🎯 Punch

From #comedy 4/7: Every manager hires their own nationality. Indian managers = Indian team. Chinese managers = Chinese team. American managers = Mexican team. When your company is all Chinese, Indian, and Mexican — "life" in work-life balance doesn't exist. These three groups = the world's hardest workers. Concept collision: diversity is just overtime with more languages.

  1. Every manager hires people from their own country. Indian boss, Indian team. Chinese boss, Chinese team. American boss… Mexican team.🔥
  2. I thought American companies have work-life balance. Then I looked around: all Chinese, Indian, and Mexican. Life left the building.🔥
  3. Those three groups are the world's hardest workers. Put them in one office and you don't get diversity — you get a sweatshop with a ping pong table.🔥
  4. Work-life balance depends on who sits next to you. Next to a French guy — two-hour lunch. Next to a Chinese guy — lunch is a crime.🔥
  5. My company celebrates every cultural holiday. Not because we're inclusive — because nobody can agree on which day to actually rest.

Your Girlfriend's Algorithm Knows Her Better Than You Do

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🧱 Build

From #comedy 4/8: "My girlfriend's TikTok is the short-video version of Pornhub." Your For You page is your real diary. You can lie to your partner but not to the algorithm. Couples live in separate algorithmic realities. Concept collision: intimacy is sharing your For You page, not your bed.

  1. My girlfriend's TikTok is the short-video version of Pornhub. I'm not judging — I'm just saying the algorithm knows things I don't.🔥
  2. Your For You page is your real diary. You can lie to your therapist, your partner, yourself. But not to the algorithm. The algorithm saw you pause.🔥
  3. True intimacy in 2026 is not moving in together. It's letting someone scroll your For You page without supervision.🔥
  4. Couples think they share a life. They share a bed. They have completely separate algorithmic realities. You're watching cooking videos. She's watching a man explain why all men are trash. Same couch. Different universes.
  5. The most dangerous question in a relationship isn't "where is this going?" It's "can I see your For You page?"

💡 Concept Seeds (for Jesse to grow)

  • Beverage × Immigration: Chinese people moved to America and switched from tea to coffee. That's not assimilation — that's surrender. You can keep your culture or keep your job. Pick one. The drink changed before the passport did.
  • Diversity Hire × Workload: The real diversity metric isn't how many nationalities you have — it's how many of them answer emails after 9 PM. Diversity of skin color: excellent. Diversity of work ethic: suspiciously uniform.
  • Algorithm × Couples Therapy: Future couples therapy won't be "tell me how you feel." It'll be "show me your search history." The therapist won't even need to talk — just compare two For You pages and diagnose the relationship in 10 seconds.
  • Posting Your Partner × Gender: Girls post their boyfriend's silhouette. Guys post their girlfriend's full retouched shoot. Same flex, different aspect ratios. One says "I'm mysterious." The other says "LOOK WHAT I CAUGHT." Both are insecurity in landscape mode.
