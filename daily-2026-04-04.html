Vibe Coding = Musical Chairs With Laptops

🆕 NEW PREMISE 💡 Thesis

From Jesse's 4/3 diary: AI makes everyone a coder now. Designers vibe code. PMs vibe code. Your CEO vibe codes. It's musical chairs — the music is AI progress, and every sprint cycle another chair disappears. Concept collision: democratized skill = devalued skill.

  1. My company told designers to learn to code. So I did. Then they gave the same tools to PMs. That's like training for a marathon and finding out everyone gets a car.🔥
  2. "Vibe coding" — where you describe what you want and AI builds it. That's not coding. That's ordering at a restaurant and calling yourself a chef.🔥
  3. AI is playing musical chairs with the entire tech industry. Except every time the music stops, the chair also learns to sit on itself.🔥
  4. They said "designers who code are the future." The future lasted six months. Now the future is "anyone with a keyboard."🔥
  5. Every job in tech is just waiting for AI to learn it. We're not employees — we're placeholder text.🔥

Designer Who Codes = Immigrant Who Assimilated Too Late

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🎯 Punch

From Jesse's 4/3 diary: at Microsoft, designers ship code directly now — no dev needed. Felt like a superpower. For one diary entry. Then realized PMs can do the same thing. Concept collision: learning to code as a designer = learning English as an immigrant — you assimilated, then they invented Google Translate.

  1. I'm a designer who learned to code. Which in 2026 is like being an immigrant who finally learned English the year they invented universal translators.🔥
  2. At my company, designers now commit code directly. No engineers needed. We disrupted the engineers. Next quarter, PMs will disrupt us. It's a food chain and we're all moving down.🔥
  3. I spent years building a bridge between design and engineering. AI just filled in the river.🔥
  4. Being a designer who codes used to be a flex. Now it's like being the last person who memorized phone numbers. Technically impressive, strategically irrelevant.🔥
  5. My competitive advantage at work has a half-life of one product cycle. I'm not building a career — I'm speedrunning obsolescence.

The 3-Minute Optimism Window

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🧱 Build

From Jesse's 4/3 diary: Entry 2 — "at our company it's more optimistic, we vibe code and ship directly!" Entry 3, literally one minute later — "but wait, PMs can do that too." The optimism lasted exactly one diary entry. Concept collision: hope as a loading state between two anxieties.

  1. I had a moment of career optimism today. It lasted one minute. I timed it. That's my personal record.🔥
  2. My brain works in three steps: anxiety → brief hope → "but actually" → deeper anxiety. Hope is just the loading screen between panics.🔥
  3. I said "at least at Microsoft we're ahead of the curve." Then my next thought was "everyone's on the same curve." I can't even finish a positive sentence without my brain filing a rebuttal.🔥
  4. Optimism for me is a transition animation. It exists only between the moment I solve one problem and the moment I realize the next one.
  5. I recorded three diary entries in two minutes. The emotional arc was: scared, relieved, scared again. That's not a diary — that's a panic attack with timestamps.🔥

💡 Concept Seeds (for Jesse to grow)

  • AI Anxiety × Immigrant Anxiety: Both are about replacement. Immigrants feared "they took our jobs." Now techies fear "it took our jobs." Same sentence, different "they." You went from being the threat to being threatened — full circle in one career.
  • Vibe Coding × Karaoke: Vibe coding is karaoke for engineers. You're not actually singing, the machine is doing the work, but you're holding the mic and feeling powerful. Until a PM grabs it and does the same song.
  • Skill Moat × Sandcastle: Every new skill you learn is a sandcastle. You build it, admire it, take a photo — and the tide of AI washes it away by next quarter. The real skill is learning to enjoy building sandcastles.
  • Microsoft × Titanic: Working at a big tech company during AI disruption = being on the Titanic but in first class. You'll still sink, but you'll have champagne and a better view.
  • Designer-Coder × Bilingual Kid: Parents made you learn two languages so you'd have an advantage. Now there's Google Translate. Parents made you learn design AND code. Now there's AI. The immigrant parent strategy keeps getting patched.
