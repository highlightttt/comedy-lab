Sam's Club / Costco = OnlyFans of Retail

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🎯 Punch

Jesse's original seed: "Sam's Club / Costco is the OnlyFans of retail." Concept collision: subscription model + exclusivity + the illusion that paying for access makes the experience special. You pay a membership fee to buy things. That's just shopping with a cover charge.

  1. Costco is the OnlyFans of retail. You pay a membership just to see what's inside. And once you're in, you spend way more than you planned.🔥
  2. Costco checks your card at the door like a bouncer. "You on the list?" Bro, I'm buying toilet paper, not entering a nightclub.🔥
  3. The Costco membership card is the only ID where the photo looks worse than your driver's license. And you show it more often.
  4. Costco sells everything in bulk because they know you came for one thing and you're leaving with a kayak. The membership fee isn't for access — it's a deposit on your self-control.🔥
  5. "I saved $200 at Costco!" No you didn't. You spent $400 to save $200. That's not saving. That's math with extra steps.🔥

The Corporate Inverse Dictionary

🆕 NEW PREMISE 💡 Thesis

Jesse's seed from 3/28: 公司总喜欢打自己的脸. Concept collision: every corporate value statement means its exact opposite. Redefine corporate vocabulary as an inverse dictionary — every word means what it doesn't say.

  1. "We encourage innovation and accept failure." Translation: "Why wasn't this caught before launch?"🔥
  2. "We're customer-centric." The customer they're centered on is called The Boss.🔥
  3. "Don't just look at the numbers — think long-term." That's what they say when the numbers are bad. When the numbers are good: "Look at the numbers!"🔥
  4. "We don't do overtime. But everyone here has ownership mentality." Ownership mentality means you work like you own the company but get paid like you don't.🔥
  5. "Flat hierarchy" means your CEO sits in the open office. Not because he believes in equality — because he wants to watch you.

Vacation = Working From Somewhere Else

🆕 NEW PREMISE 🧱 Build

Jesse's seed from 3/28: "I'm on my vacation" means "I'm working from somewhere else." Concept collision: vacation is a word that died — it now means "same laptop, different WiFi password." Plus the Chinese angle: 7 days off, 2 weekends payback.

  1. "I'm on vacation" now means "I'm working from a beach." Vacation didn't change location — work did. Vacation is just WFH with a view.🔥
  2. Chinese New Year: 7 days off, then you work the next 2 weekends to "pay it back." That's not a holiday. That's a loan with interest.🔥
  3. Americans have unlimited PTO and take 5 days a year. Chinese have 5 days of annual leave and take all 5 plus argue for a 6th. The scarcity model works better than the abundance model. Capitalism figured this out with everything except vacation.
  4. Out-of-office reply is the corporate version of "read receipts off." Everyone knows you saw the email. The auto-reply just gives you legal deniability.🔥
  5. The only real vacation is when your WiFi doesn't work. No signal = actual freedom. That's why camping exists — it's not about nature, it's about escaping Slack.

💡 Concept Seeds (for Jesse to grow)

  • Membership × Immigration: Green card is the Costco membership of countries. You pay, you wait, you get checked at the door, and once inside you still spend more than you expected.
  • Corporate Dictionary × Bilingual: Learning corporate English is harder than learning actual English. At least regular English means what it says. "Let's table this" — in English it means stop, in French it means start. Corporate language is its own country.
  • Vacation Payback × Student Loans: Chinese holiday payback weekends = vacation student loans. You borrow rest now, pay it back with interest later. Americans do the same thing — they just call it "catching up on emails."
  • Bulk Buying × Immigrant Family: Costco was designed for immigrant families. Buy 48 rolls of toilet paper? My mom already did that before Costco existed. She called it "the suitcase from China."
  • Out-of-Office × Ghosting: Setting an out-of-office is professional ghosting. "I'm currently unavailable" = "I saw your message and chose peace." The only difference is the return date.
